Grand Reserve Cabernet Sauvignon

The Grand Reserve Cabernet Sauvignon grapes are fussy and need to grow in ideal temperatures in order to remove their inherent herbaceous flavor. These particular grapes are grown on 30-year-old vines located in the high-altitdue Uco Valley, Tupungato (Elev. 3,527ft). These grapes leverage environmental advantages to boost the phenols and tannins that define the Cabernet's distinct flavor.

AGING.

13 months in small French and American Oak barrels, the bottle-aged an additional 12 months. .

TASTING NOTES.

An aromatic, complex wine with a deep ruby red color. On the nose there is a fragrant bouquet of fresh berries and cassis. On the palate there are notes of chocolate, black pepper and caramel. Finsihes with hints of fruit and vanilla.

PAIRINGS.

Steak, Chili, Sausage, sharp white cheddar, Monterey Jack (aged), lamb chop, rosemary, heavy red sauces.
